# Social Engineering Defense Lab

## Overview
I created this project to better understand how phishing and vishing work from a defensive point of view.

The main focus is to study how attackers use urgency, trust, fear, authority, and confusion to pressure people into making unsafe decisions.

This is a local-only, beginner-friendly cybersecurity portfolio project built with dummy scenarios only.

It is designed to help me practise identifying warning signs, thinking through attacker goals, and recommending safer responses.

## Why I Made This
I made this project because I wanted a practical way to learn how phishing and vishing scams actually work.

I also wanted to understand why people fall for them, especially when they are busy, stressed, or rushed.

Instead of looking at offensive tools, I wanted to focus on the defensive side: spotting red flags, understanding human pressure, and improving safe response habits.

## What I’m Learning
Through this project, I am learning how to identify phishing and vishing red flags.

I am also learning how to understand what the attacker is trying to get.

Another part of the project is learning how to analyse why a normal person might trust a suspicious message or phone call.

I also want to get better at connecting warning signs to safer defensive actions.

## Why It Matters
This matters because many attacks succeed not because they are highly technical, but because they target people under pressure.

Learning how these scams work can help me recognise them earlier.

It can also help me respond more safely and build stronger security awareness and reporting habits.
